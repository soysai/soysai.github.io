If you live in a multicultural environment...you can simply make friends! Wait.. I know what you are thinking.. "What if they think I am weird?" Trust me, it is quite the opposite. In fact, many appreciate it! They appreciate the time and effort that it took to learn their language, appreciate you reaching to them!
Ofc, don't be creepy about it, or approach them just because you are absolutely itching to speak to anyone who speaks that language and not that someone. That, my friend, is devaluing that person. Yeah, don't do that, please.
And if you live in a relatively homogenous place, there's still a buch of things you can do to learn! For instance, you can attend global fairs or cultural events. (In Bangalore, where I am from, there is an annual Japanese Hubba). Or, you could watch movies or TV shows (Youtube clips keep me engaged and listening to a lot of them allows me to slowly compute words >:]) Or... you could go online, look up audiobooks or watch "Useful Japanese words!" I could go on and on and on, but you get the gist, there are unlimite possibilities in today's age ofe globalization...

So get creative!
